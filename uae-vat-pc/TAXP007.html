TAXP007

Tax Procedures Public Clarification

Issue

Registrants must inform the FTA within 20 business days of any event that requires updating information in their tax records. Failure to do so constitutes a tax violation and results in administrative penalties.

Summary

  • Administrative penalties will not be imposed on registrants who update their tax records during the grace period from 1 January 2024 to 31 March 2025.
  • If penalties were already imposed during this period due to failure to update, they will be reversed automatically.

Detailed Analysis

Introduction

Registrants must notify the FTA of any changes requiring updates to their tax records, including:

  • Name, address, and email address.
  • Trade license activities.
  • Legal entity type, partnership agreement (for unincorporated partnerships), and articles of association or equivalent.
  • Nature of the registrant’s business.
  • The business address from which any activities are conducted.

Failure to notify the FTA within 20 business days is a violation subject to administrative penalties.

Grace Period

  • To encourage compliance, a grace period has been established from 1 January 2024 to 31 March 2025.
  • Registrants updating records during this period will not incur administrative penalties.
  • If a penalty was imposed between 1 January 2024 and the grace period implementation date, it will be reversed.
  • Where penalties have already been paid, the amount will be credited back to the registrant's tax account.
  • No action is required by registrants for reversal; the process is automatic.

Examples

The following are examples of scenarios covered under this Public Clarification:

Examples

  • Failure to notify the FTA within the prescribed timeline of opening a new branch and uploading the relevant trade licence.
  • Failure to notify the FTA within the prescribed timeline when the business moves to a new address.
  • Failure by a VAT or Excise Tax registrant to update their records before registering for Corporate Tax.
  • Registering for Corporate Tax with incorrect information and failing to correct it within the allowed period.

FTA Position

This Public Clarification outlines the FTA's interpretation and does not amend any legal provisions. It is effective from the date of implementation of the relevant legislation unless stated otherwise.

Defined Terms

  • Registrant: A taxable person issued with a Tax Registration Number (Article 1 of the Decree-Law).
  • Notification Obligation: A registrant must inform the FTA within 20 business days of changes to:
    • Name, address, and email address
    • Trade licence activities
    • Legal entity type, unincorporated partnership agreements, or equivalent
    • Nature of business
    • Business operating address

Penalties

  • Failure to notify the FTA of a required tax record change may result in an administrative penalty of AED 5,000 for the first instance and AED 10,000 for repeated violations (Cabinet Decision No. 40, Table 1, Penalty No. 5).
  • The FTA may issue a penalty assessment and notify the registrant within 5 business days of the violation (Article 24(1)(e) of the Decree-Law).
