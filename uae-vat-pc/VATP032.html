VATP032

VAT Public Clarification

Gold and Diamonds – Amendment to Tax Treatment of Making Service

  • Title: Gold and Diamonds – Amendment to Tax Treatment of Making Service

  • Note: This Clarification replaces VATP029.

Issue

  • Jewellers generally receive a consideration covering both the price of the Gold or Diamonds and the Making Service.

  • In practice, the gold/diamond price and the making charges may be shown separately or together on the tax invoice.

  • This Clarification explains the VAT treatment of Making Charges in light of the amendment to Cabinet Decision No. 25 of 2018.

Terms Used in This Clarification

  • "Gold": Gold and/or Diamonds.

  • "Making Service": The service of making or manufacturing Gold Items.

  • "Making Charge": The price charged by jewellers in return for Making Services.

Summary

  • Tax registrants supplying Gold are not required to impose VAT on Gold and Gold-based products if conditions in Cabinet Decision No. 25 are met. Instead, a reverse charge mechanism applies, whereby the registered recipient accounts for VAT.

  • The definition of "goods" under Cabinet Decision No. 25 was amended to include Making Services directly related to Gold for the period from 1 June 2018 to 31 December 2022.

  • This allowed the reverse charge to be applied to such Making Services for that limited period.

  • From 1 January 2023, the reverse charge applies only to the supply of Gold and products where Gold is the principal component.

  • If Making Services are charged separately, VAT must be applied to the service portion from 1 January 2023.

Detailed Analysis

Scope

  • This Clarification applies only to Gold and products that primarily consist of Gold and do not qualify for zero-rating. These are referred to as "Gold Items".

  • From 1 June 2018 to 31 December 2022, reverse charge could be applied to Making Services supplied with and directly connected to Gold Items.

  • Making Services are considered directly connected if the service is performed on the Gold Item being supplied.

  • The export of Gold Items and the supply of investment precious metals do not qualify for the reverse charge and are outside the scope of this Clarification.

Reverse Charge Mechanism

  • Tax registrants supplying Gold Items shall not impose VAT if the following conditions are met:

    • The recipient is registered for VAT.
    • The supplier retains a written declaration from the recipient confirming:
      • The recipient will use the Gold Item to produce/manufacture other Gold Items or resell it.
      • The recipient is VAT registered on the date of supply.
      • The recipient will account for the VAT on the Gold Item supplied.

  • If all above conditions are met, the recipient must account for VAT on the value of the Gold Items supplied and meet all related tax obligations.

  • From 1 June 2018 to 31 December 2022, the reverse charge may apply to Making Services directly related to the Gold supply.

Gold and Making Services (1 June 2018 – 31 December 2022)

  • If reverse charge was applied only to Gold Items (excluding Making Services), no revision is required in VAT returns.

  • If reverse charge was applied to both Gold Items and directly related Making Services, no revision is required in VAT returns.

  • If a voluntary disclosure was made (as per VATP029) to apply VAT on Making Services separately listed, no new disclosure is required to reverse it.

Gold and Making Services (From 1 January 2023)

  • Reverse charge mechanism no longer applies to Making Services separately listed from the Gold Item on invoices.

  • Suppliers must assess if the transaction is a Single Composite Supply or Multiple Supplies.

Single Composite Supply

  • Applies when a single price is charged for the Gold Item including Making Services and all these conditions are met:

    • The supply includes a principal component (Gold Item) and incidental services (e.g. Making Services).
    • Components are closely linked and not naturally separable.
    • Price is not separated.
    • The Gold and services are supplied by the same supplier.

  • If all conditions are met, it may qualify for reverse charge under Cabinet Decision No. 25.

  • Supplier and recipient must retain supporting evidence, including a tax invoice stating reverse charge applies to the single composite supply.

Multiple Supplies

  • Occurs when supplier charges separately for Gold and services, or lists prices separately on the invoice.

  • Each component must be treated as a separate supply with appropriate tax treatment.

  • Reverse charge applies only to the Gold Items if all conditions of Cabinet Decision No. 25 are met.

  • Supplier must apply standard VAT to services (e.g. Making Services) if they do not fall under reverse charge mechanism.

Additional Requirements for Separate Supplies

  • Where related services (e.g., Making Services) are treated as separate supplies, the supplier must issue tax invoices accordingly.
  • If the recipient is VAT registered, input tax may be recovered in line with standard input tax recovery rules.

Transitional Provisions

To determine whether supplies occurred before or after 1 January 2023, suppliers must follow the date of supply rules in the VAT legislation.

Correction of Errors

If incorrect VAT treatment was applied contrary to this clarification, the supplier must submit a voluntary disclosure. Further guidance is available in the Voluntary Disclosure User Guide.

Legal Disclaimer

This Public Clarification reflects the FTA’s position and does not amend existing legislation. It is valid from the effective date of the relevant laws unless stated otherwise.

Legislative References

  3. Cabinet Decision No. 25 of 2018 on applying VAT to Gold and Diamonds between Registrants (“Cabinet Decision No. 25”).

Defined Terms from Cabinet Decision No. 25

  • Registrant: A taxable person with a tax registration number, licensed for activities related to the Goods.
  • Supplier: A registrant supplying goods in the UAE.
  • Taxable Person: Any individual or legal entity registered or required to register for VAT under the Decree-Law.

Temporary Redefinition of “Goods” (1 June 2018 – 31 December 2022)

During this period, “Goods” was expanded to include Making Services directly connected to the supply of gold, diamonds, or products where the principal component is gold or diamonds.

Zero-Rated Provisions

  • Article 45(1): Zero-rating applies to direct/indirect export of goods/services outside the Implementing States as per the Executive Regulation.
  • Article 45(8): Zero-rating applies to the supply/import of investment precious metals. Article 36(2) of the Executive Regulation defines investment precious metals as:
    • Purity of 99% or more
    • Tradeable in global bullion markets

Conditions Under Cabinet Decision No. 25, Article 2(1)

If a registrant recipient resells or uses goods to produce/manufacture other goods, and provides a written declaration confirming:

  • The acquisition is for resale or production/manufacture purposes
  • The recipient is VAT registered on the date of supply
  • The recipient will account for VAT on the supply

Then, the supplier is not liable to calculate VAT on the goods and need not include it in their tax return.

Responsibility for VAT Calculation

The recipient must calculate VAT on the value of the goods supplied and is responsible for all related tax obligations.

Determination of Supply Type (Composite vs. Multiple)

  • Article 4(1): If a supply includes more than one component for a single price, the supplier must determine whether it is a single composite supply or multiple supplies.
  • Article 4(2): A single composite supply refers to a supply of goods or services with multiple components, determined by the overall contract and circumstances.
  • Article 4(3): A single composite supply exists when:
    • There is a principal component.
    • Other components are either incidental or necessary, not significant by themselves, but enhance the main supply.
    • Or when components are closely linked and cannot naturally be separated.
  • Article 4(4): A composite supply may also exist when:
    • The price for all components is not separately charged.
    • All components are supplied by the same supplier.
  • Article 46(1): If the supply qualifies as a composite supply, the tax treatment will follow that of the principal component.
  • Article 4(5): If a person supplies multiple components for one price but it’s not a composite supply, it will be treated as multiple supplies.
  • Article 46(2): In such cases, each component is treated as a separate supply, with its own tax treatment.
